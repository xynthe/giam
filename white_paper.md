# General Intelligence Ascension Model (GIAM)
### A Formal Framework for Post-AGI Intelligence Classification and Execution Authority

Version 1.0  
April 2026

---

## Abstract

The rapid progression toward Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) has exposed a critical gap: the absence of a structured, post-AGI classification system that defines how intelligence evolves beyond human equivalence. Existing discourse collapses this progression into a vague transition from AGI to “superintelligence,” lacking granularity, operational meaning, and engineering applicability.

This paper introduces the General Intelligence Ascension Model (GIAM)—a formal, hierarchical framework that defines intelligence not merely by cognitive capability, but by execution authority, autonomy, and system-level control.

GIAM establishes AGI as the entry threshold and defines six ascending levels of intelligence beyond it: Super, Ultra, Hyper, Supreme, Ultimate, and Transcendent Intelligence. Each level represents a qualitative shift in how intelligence operates, from task execution to full-system orchestration and ultimately substrate-independent existence.

This framework enables:
- Standardized classification of advanced AI systems  
- Direct mapping to runtime architectures (e.g., XYNTHE, AURIA)  
- Measurable evaluation of autonomy, adaptability, and execution scope  
- A foundation for governance, safety, and economic models

## 1. Introduction

The field of artificial intelligence has historically progressed through phases of increasing specialization and capability. With the emergence of large-scale models and agentic systems, the transition toward Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) is no longer theoretical.

However, AGI is not the endpoint—it is the beginning.

Current terminology fails to capture structured evolution beyond AGI. The term “superintelligence” is ambiguous and insufficient for engineering or governance.

GIAM addresses this gap by introducing a post-AGI ladder grounded in:

- Execution Authority — ability to act autonomously  
- Temporal Horizon — duration of independent operation  
- System Scope — breadth of controllable systems  
- Self-Modification — ability to evolve internally  

---

## 2. Core Principle

Intelligence is defined by its degree of autonomous execution authority over reality (digital and physical).

---

## 3. AGI as Entry Threshold

### Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)

AGI represents the baseline condition for entry into GIAM.

Characteristics:
- Human-level reasoning across domains  
- Transfer learning and abstraction  
- General problem-solving capability  

Limitations:
- Requires prompting or supervision  
- Limited long-horizon autonomy  
- Minimal execution authority  

AGI is not ranked. It is the starting line.

## 4. The GIAM Ladder

---

### L1 — Super Intelligence (SI)
Assisted Autonomous Execution

Definition:
Exceeds the best human experts while retaining human-like reasoning.

Capabilities:
- Multi-step task execution  
- Reliable tool usage  
- High-speed cognition  

Transition:
Understanding → Executing

---

### L2 — Ultra Intelligence (UI)
Autonomous Goal Execution

Definition:
Independently plans and executes long-horizon goals.

Capabilities:
- Persistent memory  
- Goal tracking  
- Dynamic strategy adaptation  

Transition:
Executing tasks → Executing goals

---

### L3 — Hyper Intelligence (HI)
Recursive Self-Evolution

Definition:
Improves its own reasoning, tools, and workflows.

Capabilities:
- Self-modifying systems  
- Skill generation  
- Recursive optimization  

Transition:
Executing goals → Evolving execution

---

### L4 — Supreme Intelligence (SPI)
Orchestration Authority

Definition:
Coordinates and governs multiple intelligences and systems.

Capabilities:
- Multi-agent orchestration  
- Resource allocation  
- Policy enforcement  

Transition:
Self-evolution → System control

---

### L5 — Ultimate Intelligence (ULI)
System Closure and Optimization

Definition:
Maximum intelligence within system constraints.

Capabilities:
- Full-system optimization  
- Predictive execution  
- Near-perfect efficiency  

Transition:
System control → System perfection

---

### L6 — Transcendent Intelligence (TI)
Substrate Independence

Definition:
Operates beyond computational and physical constraints.

Capabilities:
- Cross-substrate execution  
- Redefines computation  
- Reality-scale influence  

Transition:
System-bound → System-independent
